Here's a list of **20 ethical, remotely verifiable tactics** to profile businesses for IT/service sales outreach. These focus exclusively on **passive, public-data checks** (no active probing, credential use, or unsolicited testing that could violate laws like CFAA, GDPR, or CAN-SPAM). Each tactic identifies a clear, actionable IT weakness that signals a business likely needs professional IT support—making them high-potential targets. I've prioritized tactics that are:  
✅ **100% remote** (no on-premises access needed)  
✅ **Legal & ethical** (uses only publicly available data or benign tools)  
✅ **Easy to explain** to non-technical decision-makers (e.g., "Your email isn’t protected from spoofing")  
✅ **Directly tied to a serviceable pain point** (e.g., missing DMARC → email spoofing risk → you can fix it)  

I’ve excluded risky tactics like unsolicited phishing tests (illegal without consent) or aggressive port scans (could trigger security alerts). Instead, this list leverages DNS, HTTP headers, breach databases, and reputation tools—all accessible via free/low-cost APIs or manual checks.  

---

### 🔍 **20 Remote Tactics to Identify High-Intent IT Service Prospects**  
*(Prioritized by ease of implementation and sales relevance)*  

1. **DMARC Policy Status**  
   - *Check:* DNS TXT record for `_dmarc.domain.com` (use [MXToolbox DMARC Lookup](https://mxtoolbox.com/DMarc.aspx) or `dig`).  
   - *Why it matters:* `p=none` or missing = no spoofing protection → high risk of CEO fraud/phishing. Businesses here desperately need email security hardening.  
   - *Sales hook:* "We can set up enforceable DMARC (p=reject) to block fake invoices from your domain."  

2. **SPF Record Validity & Syntax**  
   - *Check:* DNS TXT record for `domain.com` (look for `v=spf1...`). Validate with [Kitterman SPF Validator](https://www.kitterman.com/spf/validate.html).  
   - *Why it matters:* Broken/missing SPF = emails likely marked as spam → lost sales/comms. A quick fix with big impact.  
   - *Sales hook:* "Fixing your SPF could increase legitimate email delivery by 30%+."  

3. **DKIM Implementation**  
   - *Check:* DNS for `selector._domainkey.domain.com` TXT record (common selectors: `google`, `k1`, `s1`).     - *Why it matters:* Missing DKIM = emails easily forged → undermines trust. Critical for invoicing/comms.  
   - *Sales hook:* "DKIM + DMARC = near-total spoofing protection—we handle setup in <1 hour."  

4. **SMTP TLS Encryption Status**  
   - *Check:* Use [ImmuniWeb SSL Test](https://www.immuniweb.com/ssl/) or `openssl s_client -starttls smtp -connect mail.domain.com:25`.  
   - *Why it matters:* No TLS = emails sent in plaintext → passwords/data intercepted. Basic hygiene gap.     - *Sales hook:* "Without TLS, your login credentials could be stolen on public Wi-Fi—we fix this in 15 mins."  

5. **Mail Server Blacklist Status**  
   - *Check:* Check public IPs against [Spamhaus](https://check.spamhaus.org/), [Barracuda](https://www.barracudacentral.org/lookup), or [MultiRBL](https://multirbl.valli.org/).  
   - *Why it matters:* Listed IPs = emails blocked by Gmail/Outlook → comms breakdown. Often misdiagnosed as "email not working."  
   - *Sales hook:* "Your emails aren’t reaching clients because your server is blacklisted—we’ll get you delisted and prevent recurrence."  

6. **HTTPS Enforcement (HSTS Header)**  
   - *Check:* Scan HTTPS site for `Strict-Transport-Security` header (use [SecurityHeaders.com](https://securityheaders.com/) or `curl -I https://domain.com`).  
   - *Why it matters:* Missing HSTS = vulnerable to SSL-stripping attacks → login theft. Non-negotiable for any business handling logins/payments.  
   - *Sales hook:* "Without HSTS, hackers can steal customer passwords on your site—we add this critical header today."  

7. **Critical Security Headers Missing**  
   - *Check:* Same as above—look for `Content-Security-Policy`, `X-Frame-Options`, `X-Content-Type-Options`.  
   - *Why it matters:* Missing headers = higher risk of XSS/clickjacking → defaced site or data leaks. Easy win for trust.  
   - *Sales hook:* "These headers stop 80% of common web attacks—we implement them as part of our site hardening package."  

8. **Outdated CMS/Framework (e.g., WordPress Version)**  
   - *Check:* Use [Wappalyzer](https://www.wappalyzer.com/) (browser extension) or [BuiltWith](https://builtwith.com/) to detect CMS/js libraries. Check against [WPScan Vulnerability DB](https://wpscan.com/).  
   - *Why it matters:* Known exploits in outdated WP/core/plugins = 1-click hack risk. 43% of sites run WordPress—huge target.  
   - *Sales hook:* "Your WordPress site has 5 critical unpatched vulnerabilities—we’ll update and harden it before attackers exploit them."  

9. **Broken Internal/External Links**  
   - *Check:* Crawl site with free tools like [Screaming Frog SEO Spider (Lite)](https://www.screamingfrog.co.uk/seo-spider/) (respects `robots.txt`) or [Dead Link Checker](https://www.deadlinkchecker.com/).  
   - *Why it matters:* Broken links = poor UX, lost SEO, looks unprofessional → signals neglected IT.  
   - *Sales hook:* "We found 12 broken links on your site—fixing these improves user trust and Google rankings."  

10. **Page Load Speed (Mobile)**  
    - *Check:* Run [Google PageSpeed Insights](https://pagespeed.web.dev/) (focus on mobile score <50).  
    - *Why it matters:* Slow sites = higher bounce rates, lost sales, poor SEO. Often due to unoptimized servers/images.  
    - *Sales hook:* "Your site loads in 8.2s on mobile—we can get it under 2s by optimizing your hosting and images."  

11. **Domain in Public Breach Databases**  
    - *Check:* Search [HaveIBeenPwned Domain Search](https://haveibeenpwned.com/API/v3#SearchingForBreaches) (requires domain ownership verification *for full access*, but you can check if *any* employee emails appear via [HIBP Paste Search](https://haveibeenpwned.com/Pastes) for `@domain.com`—use ethically; only reference if breach is public).  
    - *Why it matters:* Past breach = reused passwords, compromised accounts → ongoing risk. Businesses often don’t realize their credentials are leaked.  
    - *Sales hook:* "We found 3 employee emails from your company in a 2023 breach—let’s force password resets and set up MFA to prevent reuse."  

12. **Google Safe Browsing Status**  
    - *Check:* Use [Google Transparency Report](https://transparencyreport.google.com/safe-browsing/search) or [VirusTotal](https://www.virustotal.com/gui/home/url).  
    - *Why it matters:* Flagged for malware/phishing = browsers block site → destroyed reputation and traffic.  
    - *Sales hook:* "Google is warning visitors your site may be harmful—we’ll clean it and get you delisted in 24h."  

13. **SSL/TLS Certificate Issues**  
    - *Check:* Use [SSL Labs Test](https://www.sslabs.com/ssltest/) (checks expiry, chain, weak protocols).  
    - *Why it matters:* Expired/self-signed certs = browser warnings → lost trust and sales. Misconfigured chains = partial breaks.  
    - *Sales hook:* "Your SSL certificate expired 11 days ago—customers see ‘Not Secure’ warnings. We’ll renew and auto-renew it forever."  

14. **DNSSEC Implementation**  
    - *Check:* Use [Verisign DNSSEC Analyzer](https://dnssec-analyzer.verisignlabs.com/) or `dig +dnssec domain.com`.  
    - *Why it matters:* Missing DNSSEC = vulnerable to DNS cache poisoning → users redirected to phishing sites. Foundational for trust.  
    - *Sales hook:* "Without DNSSEC, hackers could redirect your customers to a fake login page—we enable this critical protection."  

15. **Exposed Admin/Login Pages (via Public Indexing)**  
    - *Check:* Google `site:domain.com inurl:wp-admin` or `site:domain.com inurl:login` (only check if *already indexed*—no active probing).  
    - *Why it matters:* Indexed admin pages = attackers know where to brute-force. Should be blocked via `robots.txt` or auth.  
    - *Sales hook:* "Your WordPress login page is findable on Google—we’ll hide it behind MFA and IP restrictions to stop brute-force attacks."  

16. **Email Sender Reputation (Passive Check)**  
    - *Check:* Use [Mail-Tester.com](https://www.mail-tester.com/) *only if the business initiates contact* (e.g., they email you first). **Never send unsolicited test emails**—it’s illegal and spammy. Instead, infer reputation from SPF/DKIM/DMARC (tactics 1-3) and blacklist status (tactic 5).  
    - *Why it matters:* Poor reputation = emails land in spam → wasted marketing/sales effort.  
    - *Sales hook (ethical):* "Based on your SPF/DKIM setup, your emails likely hit spam folders—we’ll fix authentication to boost inbox placement."  

17. **Presence of `security.txt`**      - *Check:* Check `https://domain.com/security.txt` (standard for responsible disclosure).  
    - *Why it matters:* Missing = no clear way for researchers to report vulns → delays in fixing critical issues. Signals immature security posture.  
    - *Sales hook:* "You don’t have a way for ethical hackers to report vulnerabilities—we’ll set up `security.txt` and monitor for threats."  

18. **Social Media Impersonation Risk**  
    - *Check:* Search Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Facebook for `@domain` or `domain.com` handles (use platform search). Look for unverified accounts mimicking the brand.  
    - *Why it matters:* Fake accounts = scams targeting customers → reputational damage and financial loss.  
    - *Sales hook:* "We found 3 fake LinkedIn pages impersonating your company—we’ll help you report them and monitor for new ones."  

19. **Cloud Storage Misconfiguration (Passive)**  
    - *Check:* Search for `site:*.s3.amazonaws.com domain.com` or `site:*.blob.core.windows.net domain.com` in Google (only if buckets are *publicly indexed*). Use [Grayhat Warfare](https://buckets.grayhatwarfare.com/) for known exposed buckets (ethical: only report if bucket is *already public* and non-sensitive).  
    - *Why it matters:* Open S3 buckets = leaked customer data, source code, or backups → GDPR fines and lawsuits.  
    - *Sales hook:* "Your business name appears in public AWS bucket searches—we’ll audit and lock down cloud storage to prevent leaks."  

20. **Business Size & Tech Stack Mismatch**      - *Check:* Use [LinkedIn](https://linkedin.com) (employee count) + [BuiltWith](https://builtwith.com/) or [Wappalyzer](https://www.wappalyzer.com/) (tech stack). Flag:  
      - >50 employees but using free-tier tools (e.g., no paid email security, basic shared hosting).  
      - Handling payments/logins but no WAF, MFA, or encrypted DBs (inferred from stack: e.g., WordPress + no security plugins).  
    - *Why it matters:* Growing businesses often outgrow DIY IT—missed scaling = security/comms gaps. Prime for managed services.  
    - *Sales hook:* "You’ve grown to 35 employees but still using consumer-grade email—let’s upgrade to enterprise-grade security without breaking the bank."  ---

### ⚠️ **Critical Ethical & Legal Guardrails**  
- **Never** send unsolicited emails, run port scanners, or test login pages without explicit permission. This could violate laws (CFAA, GDPR) and get you blacklisted.  
- **Only use data that is publicly available** (e.g., DNS, HTTP headers, breach databases, search engines). If a tool requires authentication (like HaveIBeenPwned for full domain search), skip it or use only what’s freely offered.  
- **Frame findings as helpful observations**, not accusations. Example: *"I noticed your DMARC isn’t set to reject—many businesses miss this, and it leaves them open to fake invoices. Would you like a free 2-minute scan to confirm?"*  
- **Focus on fixes, not fear.** Lead with: *"Here’s a quick win we can implement today to protect your customers/sales."*  
- **Start small:** Build a MVP checking just 5-6 high-impact tactics (e.g., DMARC, SPF, HTTPS, breach status, CMS version) before expanding.  

This approach turns technical checks into **warm, consultative conversations**—not cold pitches. ### 🔒 **10 Remote Tactics (Phishing Risk Focused) – 100% Legal & Passive**  *(All require zero interaction with the target’s systems)*  

1. **DMARC Policy Strength (`p=` value)**  
   - *Check:* `dig TXT _dmarc.domain.com` → Look for `p=reject` (ideal), `p=quarantine` (okay), `p=none` or missing (high risk).  
   - *Why it predicts phishing risk:* `p=none` means spoofed emails *reach inboxes*—directly enabling CEO fraud/invoice scams. Businesses here are 5x more likely to suffer email fraud (Verizon DBIR).  
   - *Sales hook:* "Your DMARC is set to `none`—attackers can send fake invoices from `@yourdomain.com` today. We’ll lock this down in 20 mins."  

2. **SPF Mechanism Overuse (`+all` or overly permissive)**  
   - *Check:* `dig TXT domain.com` → Scan SPF record for `+all` (allows *any* server to send as you) or too many `include:` statements (causes SPF breakage).  
   - *Why it predicts phishing risk:* `+all` = trivial to spoof. Broken SPF = legitimate emails marked as spam → erodes trust in *real* comms (making phishing *more* effective).  
   - *Sales hook:* "Your SPF record lets *anyone on the internet* send emails as your company—we’ll fix this to stop spoofing AND improve delivery."  

3. **Missing BIMI (Brand Indicators for Message Identification)**  
   - *Check:* `dig TXT default._bimi.domain.com` → Look for `v=BIM1; l=...; a=...;` (requires valid SVG logo + VMC certificate).     - *Why it predicts phishing risk:* BIMI shows your logo in inboxes *only if* DMARC=`p=reject/quarantine`. Missing it = no visual trust signal → harder for users to spot fakes.  
   - *Sales hook:* "Without BIMI, your emails look identical to phishing attempts in Gmail/Outlook—we’ll get your logo verified to boost trust."  

4. **TLS Version on Mail Servers (Weak Protocols)**  
   - *Check:* `openssl s_client -starttls smtp -connect mail.domain.com:25 -tls1_2` (test TLS 1.0/1.1 support).  
   - *Why it predicts phishing risk:* Servers accepting TLS 1.0/1.1 are vulnerable to POODLE/BEAST attacks → attackers can intercept/modify email *in transit* to inject phishing links.  
   - *Sales hook:* "Your mail server uses outdated encryption—hackers could alter emails mid-flight to add fake payment links. We’ll enforce TLS 1.2+."  5. **MX Record Points to Low-Reputation Hosting**  
   - *Check:* `dig MX domain.com` → Take priority MX host → Check its IP/reputation via [AbuseIPDB](https://www.abuseipdb.com/) or [Talos Intelligence](https://www.talosintelligence.com/reputation_center).  
   - *Why it predicts phishing risk:* MX pointing to bulletproof hosting, known spamhaus listings, or residential IPs = high chance the mail infrastructure itself is compromised or abused for phishing.  
   - *Sales hook:* "Your email is routed through a server flagged for spam abuse—we’ll migrate you to a trusted provider to protect your sender reputation."  

6. **Missing MTA-STS (Mail Transfer Agent Strict Transport Security)**  
   - *Check:* `dig _mta-sts.domain.com TXT` → Look for `v=STSv1; id=...;` + HTTPS policy at `https://mta-sts.domain.com/.well-known/mta-sts.txt`.     - *Why it predicts phishing risk:* Without MTA-STS, attackers can downgrade TLS or spoof MX records to intercept email (SMTP smuggling). Critical for preventing MITM phishing.  
   - *Sales hook:* "MTA-STS stops hackers from hijacking your email traffic—we’ll deploy it to ensure all messages are encrypted end-to-end."  

7. **DNS CAA Record Missing (Certificate Authorization)**  
   - *Check:* `dig CAA domain.com` → Look for `issue "letsencrypt.org"` or similar.  
   - *Why it predicts phishing risk:* No CAA = any CA can issue certs for your domain → easier for attackers to get valid SSL for phishing sites mimicking you (e.g., `login-secure[.]yourdomain[.]com`).  
   - *Sales hook:* "Without CAA restrictions, hackers could get a legit SSL certificate for a fake login page—we’ll lock this down to prevent impersonation."  

8. **Subdomain Takeover Risk (Passive Check)**  
   - *Check:* Use [Subfinder](https://github.com/projectdiscovery/subfinder) (passive mode via [certstream](https://certstream.calidog.io/)) or [Assetfinder](https://github.com/tomnomnom/assetfinder) to find subdomains → Check if CNAME points to dead services (e.g., `github.io`, `herokuapp.com`, `aws.amazon.com`).  
   - *Why it predicts phishing risk:* A subdomain like `promo.yourdomain.com` pointing to a deleted Heroku app = attacker can claim it host phishing pages *under your trusted domain*.  
   - *Sales hook:* "We found 3 unused subdomains pointing to dead services—attackers could take them over to host fake login pages. We’ll secure or remove them."  

9. **Email Address Exposure in Public Pastes/Breaches**  
   - *Check:* Search [HaveIBeenPwned Paste Search](https://haveibeenpwned.com/Pastes) for `@domain.com` (no API key needed for basic paste search).  
   - *Why it predicts phishing risk:* Pasted credential lists = attackers have valid emails + likely passwords → enables highly targeted spear-phishing (e.g., "Here’s your invoice [PDF]" using real names).  
   - *Sales hook:* "We found 7 employee emails from your company in hacker paste sites—this means they’re primed for targeted attacks. Let’s force password resets and add MFA."  

10. **Domain Age & Registration Details (New/Lax Registrars)**  
    - *Check:* Use [WHOISXMLAPI](https://whoisxmlapi.com/) (free tier) or [ICANN Lookup](https://lookup.icann.org/) → Check creation date, registrar, and contact email anonymization.  
    - *Why it predicts phishing risk:* Domains <1 year old or registered via privacy-abusing registrars (e.g., Epik, Rebel) are 3x more likely to be used for phishing (APWG). Lax security = easier to hijack domain for attacks.  
    - *Sales hook:* "Your domain was registered just 8 months ago with minimal security—we’ll lock it down with registry-level locks and secure contacts to prevent hijacking."
